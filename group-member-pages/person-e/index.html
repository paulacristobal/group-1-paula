Oakland's Encampment Abatement Policy — A Community Divided

Reported from city council chambers from early-morning sessions to midnight public comments, this investigation brought together the voices of unhoused residents, business owners, and policymakers to interrogate whether compassion and enforcement could coexist in Oakland.

"Ashley Greene had lived in a tent near 98th Avenue for over a year and had been on shelter waitlists for months. Kevin Hester said his East Oakland construction business was on the verge of leaving." — Field Reporting, Oakland

Through months of relationship-building and trust-earning interviews, the story revealed systemic gaps in shelter access and the human cost of policy decisions made in council chambers far from the street.

Data Journalism / Public Health Multimedia Project

Oakland's Silent Killer — The Drug Overdose Crisis

A multimedia investigation combining street-level reporting with independent statistical analysis of Alameda County drug use and death data. By interviewing people living through addiction, harm-reduction workers, and public health experts, the project transformed county-wide numbers into a human portrait of the overdose epidemic.

The work demonstrated that the most powerful data journalism is built on community trust, not spreadsheets alone.
